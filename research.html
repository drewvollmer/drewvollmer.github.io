Selling with Demand Uncertainty
(Job Market Paper)

Abstract: What are the profit- and welfare-maximizing sales strategies for a seller of perishable goods facing demand uncertainty? I use a structural model of college football ticket sales that includes primary and resale markets to evaluate three sales strategies: resale, refunds, and a menu of refunds. The optimal strategy depends on the type of demand uncertainty, specifically whether shocks are idiosyncratic or aggregate and whether all consumers face the same degree of uncertainty. The model includes three sources of uncertainty: idiosyncratic shocks, an aggregate shock that affect consumers equally, and an aggregate shock that has a heterogeneous effect on consumers. I estimate the model using primary and resale market data on ticket sales as well as survey data on demand for tickets. I find that using refunds rather than resale raises profit by 3.6% and total welfare by 1.3%. Both strategies are better than not reallocating, with refunds producing gains of 5.5% for profit and 6.2% for total welfare. Screening with a menu of refunds substantially increases profit and welfare, with gains of 10.6% for profit and 6.4% for total welfare.
